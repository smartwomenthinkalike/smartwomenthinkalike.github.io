About the society

Smart Women Think Alike was founded at Durham in 2025 on a simple belief: true personal growth comes from connections across disciplines. University makes it easy to socialise only within your own subject, yet the working world is moving the other way. With the rise of AI reshaping how companies operate, disciplines are crossing paths more than ever: tech with medicine, finance with law.

The society brings women together across those boundaries, through career events that go beyond a speech. We interview our speakers, asking senior professionals what it's actually like to build a career, and to be a woman in a male-dominated field. In its first year, SWTA interviewed professionals from Microsoft, Aviva and the AI Economy Institute, covering everything from neuroscience and STEM representation to strategic transformation and leadership in law.

The society exists to create a supportive space where ambitious women can learn, lead and connect beyond academic and professional boundaries, and to pass that space on, intact and growing, from one exec to the next. That's what the archive below is for: each committee leaves a record of its year, so the society's memory is longer than any one exec's tenure.
